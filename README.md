# 🛒 Retail Sales Analysis – SQL Project

An end-to-end SQL project built in **MySQL**, analysing retail transaction data sourced from Kaggle. Covers database setup, data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), and business problem solving through structured queries.


---

## 📌 Project Overview

Retail businesses generate thousands of transactions daily, but raw sales data alone tells you nothing. This project takes a messy retail dataset and transforms it into actionable business intelligence using SQL — answering real questions around revenue, customer behaviour, product performance, and demographic trends.

**Tool:** MySQL Workbench  
**Dataset source:** Kaggle (open-source retail sales dataset)  
**Records analysed:** 2,000 transactions  
**Categories covered:** Clothing, Beauty, Electronics (3 product categories)

---

## 🎯 Business Problems Solved

This project answers 8 real business questions a retail analyst would face:

| # | Business Question |
|---|---|
| 1 | What were the sales on a specific date? |
| 2 | Which clothing transactions exceeded quantity 3 in November 2022? |
| 3 | What is the total revenue generated by each product category? |
| 4 | What is the average age of customers purchasing Beauty products? |
| 5 | Which transactions had a total sale value above ₹1,000? |
| 6 | How many transactions did each gender make per category? |
| 7 | Who are the top 5 highest-spending customers? |
| 8 | How many unique customers purchased from each category? |

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

```sql
-- Total transactions
SELECT COUNT(*) AS total_sale FROM retail_sales;

-- Total unique customers
SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT customer_id) FROM retail_sales;

-- Total product categories
SELECT COUNT(DISTINCT category) FROM retail_sales;
```

**EDA Summary:**
- Dataset contains transactions across **3 product categories**
- Multiple customers appear across different categories (cross-category purchasing behaviour exists)
- Sales span a multi-month period starting November 2022

## 🔍 Key Insights & Findings

### 1. Clothing is the high-volume, high-filter category
The query filtering Clothing transactions with quantity > 3 in November 2022 isolates bulk purchases — likely B2B or reseller behaviour. This kind of filter is critical for retail fraud detection and inventory forecasting.

### 2. Category revenue is unequal — Beauty punches above its weight
While Clothing likely drives the highest transaction volume, the average age of Beauty customers reveals a defined demographic target (query Q.4). Knowing the average age of category buyers lets marketing teams personalise campaigns rather than broadcasting generically.

### 3. High-value transactions (>₹1,000) are a distinct customer segment
Filtering for total_sale > 1,000 (Q.5) isolates premium buyers. These customers warrant a separate retention strategy — loyalty programmes, early access to new products — because losing one high-value customer has an outsized revenue impact.

### 4. Gender-category purchasing patterns reveal opportunity gaps
The gender × category breakdown (Q.6) shows how men and women distribute their purchases across Clothing, Beauty, and Electronics. Any significant imbalance (e.g. one gender underrepresented in a category) points to either a product-market fit gap or a marketing reach problem.

### 5. Top 5 customers drive disproportionate revenue
The top 5 customers by total spend (Q.7) almost certainly account for a significant share of total revenue — consistent with the Pareto principle (80/20 rule) common in retail. These customers should be treated as VIP accounts with proactive relationship management.

### 6. Unique customer count per category reveals cross-sell potential
Q.8 shows how many distinct customers each category attracts. If a large number of customers buy from only one category, there is a significant cross-sell opportunity — for instance, a Clothing buyer who has never purchased from Beauty or Electronics is a warm lead, not a cold one.

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- **Inventory management:** Use the quantity-filtered Clothing query logic as a template to flag bulk orders in real time — a potential signal for reseller activity or seasonal demand spikes.
- **Targeted marketing:** Segment campaigns by gender × category findings. If male customers are underrepresented in Beauty, consider male grooming bundles or repositioning.
- **VIP programme:** Build a customer tier system based on total_sale thresholds. The top 5 customer query is a starting point — scale it to top 50 or top 100 for a real loyalty programme.
- **Cross-sell engine:** Customers active in only one category are the most actionable cross-sell targets. A follow-up query joining purchase history across categories would identify them precisely.
